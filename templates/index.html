Who in Congress are the best stock pickers?

This site reconstructs every stock trade disclosed by members of the U.S. House and Senate under the STOCK Act over the trailing two years, then asks a simple question: if you had copied each member into their trades on the day the trade became public, whose trades would have made you the most money? Members are ranked by the dollar-weighted return of that "follow the disclosure" strategy, benchmarked against {{ benchmark }}. From the leaderboard you can see which stocks the out-performing members are buying — especially the names bought by several of them in size — and drill into any company.

About the method

  • Data. House PTRs from the Clerk's annual disclosure index + PDFs; Senate PTRs from the eFD electronic filings. Equity (stock) transactions only.
  • Entry. Each purchase is priced at the closing price on/after its public disclosure date — the first day you could have acted on it.
  • Exit. A sale closes the member's entire open position in that ticker at the sale's disclosure-date close; positions never sold are held to the present.
  • Ranking. Dollar-weighted by the midpoint of each disclosed amount range, so larger bets count more. Alpha is the return above {{ benchmark }} over the same window.
  • Enrichment. Company description, 2-year price chart, recent news, and key financials come from Polygon.io.
